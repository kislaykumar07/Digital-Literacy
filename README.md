## What's This About

This is my submission for the Student Digital Ambassador program, done as part of the CSE0001 course. The idea behind it was pretty straightforward — go from just *using* the internet to actually understanding how to use it well, professionally, and safely.

Across five tasks, I worked on things like building an online presence, practicing code, writing professional emails, and learning about cybersecurity. What surprised me most was how much of this stuff I was already doing wrong (or just not doing at all).

---

## Project Breakdown

### Task 1 — Digital Literacy Infographic
Made an infographic on Canva explaining what digital literacy actually means — not the textbook definition, but the practical stuff students actually need. Covered three main areas: what digital literacy is, tools every student should know about, and why things like Multi-Factor Authentication matter.

### Task 2 — Student Digital Portfolio
Set up profiles on GitHub, LinkedIn, and Kaggle. The GitHub one has a README that describes my academic interests, and LinkedIn is updated with my current degree info at VIT Bhopal. Basically started building a professional presence from scratch.

### Task 3 — Coding & Collaboration Platforms
Did some hands-on work here. Completed the "Solve Me First" Python challenge on HackerRank to get comfortable with competitive coding platforms. Also built a 5-question Digital Literacy Quiz using Google Forms and connected it to a Google Sheet to track responses automatically.

### Task 4 — Email Etiquette & Social Media
Drafted two professional emails — one asking for an assignment deadline extension, the other reaching out about an internship. Also put together a "Social Media Do's and Don'ts" checklist covering things that seem obvious but a lot of people get wrong online.

### Task 5 — Cybercrime Awareness
Researched phishing as a case study — how it works, why people fall for it, and what to actually do about it. Created an 8-point "Stay Safe Online" checklist with a focus on UPI safety and the National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal (helpline: **1930**).
